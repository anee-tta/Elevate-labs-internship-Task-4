How the Firewall Filters Traffic

` Windows Defender Firewall evaluates each packet against a prioritized set of rules.
` Rules are matched by criteria such as:
  ` Direction (inbound/outbound)
  ` Protocol (TCP/UDP)
  ` Local/remote port and IP address
  ` Profile (Domain/Private/Public)
` When a packet matches a rule, the firewall applies that rule’s action.
` If no rule matches, the default policy applies .
` By creating a specific “Block” rule for port 23, we forced the firewall to drop inbound Telnet traffic regardless of other rules, and then removed that rule to restore normal behavior.
